The Legend of Tinajón

"If you drink the water from the Tinajón, you shall always return to Camagüey."

These iconic clay vessels are the silent witnesses of our history. Originally designed to store rainwater in the dry savannahs, they have become the ultimate symbol of regional identity.

The Labyrinth City

Unlike any other Spanish colonial town, Camagüey was built as a maze of irregular streets and blind alleys to confuse pirates and invaders. Today, it serves as an open-air museum of architecture and community.
